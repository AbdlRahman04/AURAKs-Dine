Smart Cafeteria Ordering System - Requirements Specification
Functional Requirements​
Functional requirements describe the services the system should provide, how the system should react to particular inputs, and how the system should behave in particular situations. For the Smart Cafeteria Ordering System, the following detailed functional requirements have been identified:​

Authentication and User Management
FR-01: The system shall allow students to register for an account by providing their student ID, name, email address, and password.​

FR-02: The system shall authenticate registered students through a secure login process using their email/student ID and password.​

FR-03: The system shall allow students to reset their password through email verification if forgotten.​

FR-04: Each student using the system shall be uniquely identified by their 8-digit student ID number.​

FR-05: The system shall allow students to view and update their profile information including contact details and preferred pickup locations.​

Menu Browsing and Display
FR-06: The system shall display the daily menu with food items organized by categories (breakfast, lunch, snacks, beverages).​

FR-07: Each menu item shall display the name, description, price, image, availability status, and preparation time.​

FR-08: Students shall be able to search for specific menu items by name or filter by category, price range, or dietary preferences.​

FR-09: The system shall display real-time availability status of menu items to prevent orders for unavailable items.​

FR-10: Students shall be able to view nutritional information and allergen warnings for each menu item.​

Meal Pre-Ordering
FR-11: The system shall allow students to add menu items to a shopping cart with quantity selection and customization options.​

FR-12: Students shall be able to modify or delete items from their cart before confirming the order.​

FR-13: The system shall allow students to specify a pickup time slot when placing their pre-order, with minimum 15-minute advance notice.​

FR-14: The system shall calculate and display the total order cost including any applicable taxes before order confirmation.​

FR-15: Students shall be able to add special instructions or dietary requirements to their orders.​

FR-16: The system shall generate a unique order number for each confirmed order.​

FR-17: Students shall receive an order confirmation with order details, pickup time, and order number immediately after placing an order.​

FR-18: Students shall be able to cancel their order if it has not yet been prepared, within a specified timeframe.​

Order Tracking and Notifications
FR-19: The system shall allow students to view the real-time status of their orders (received, preparing, ready for pickup, completed).​

FR-20: The system shall send notifications to students when their order status changes (confirmed, ready for pickup).​

FR-21: Students shall be able to track their order preparation progress through the mobile/web interface.​

FR-22: Students shall be able to view their complete order history with dates, items ordered, and total amounts.​

Payment Processing
FR-23: The system shall support multiple payment methods including credit/debit cards, digital wallets, and campus card integration.​

FR-24: The system shall process payments securely through encrypted payment gateway integration.​

FR-25: Students shall receive a digital receipt after successful payment.​

FR-26: The system shall allow students to save preferred payment methods for future orders.​

FR-27: The system shall verify student eligibility for payroll deduction or campus card payment before accepting orders.​

Administrative Interface
FR-28: The system shall provide an admin interface accessible only to authorized cafeteria staff.​

FR-29: Cafeteria administrators shall be able to add new menu items with name, description, price, category, image, and availability status.​

FR-30: Administrators shall be able to update existing menu items including modifying prices, descriptions, and availability.​

FR-31: Administrators shall be able to delete menu items that are permanently discontinued.​

FR-32: The system shall allow administrators to set daily specials and promotional offers.​

FR-33: Administrators shall be able to temporarily mark menu items as unavailable without deleting them.​

Order Management for Staff
FR-34: The system shall display all incoming orders to cafeteria staff in real-time on a kitchen display system.​

FR-35: Staff shall be able to update order status as orders progress through preparation stages.​

FR-36: The system shall display orders organized by pickup time to help staff prioritize preparation.​

FR-37: Staff shall be able to view order details including all items, quantities, special instructions, and customer information.​

FR-38: The system shall alert staff when orders are approaching their scheduled pickup time.​

Reporting and Analytics
FR-39: The system shall generate daily reports showing total orders, revenue, and popular menu items.​

FR-40: The system shall generate weekly reports summarizing sales trends, peak ordering times, and inventory needs.​

FR-41: Administrators shall be able to view reports on order fulfillment rates and average preparation times.​

FR-42: The system shall provide analytics on food waste by tracking items that were unavailable or orders that were cancelled.​

FR-43: The system shall allow administrators to export reports in CSV or PDF format.​

Favorites and Recommendations
FR-44: Students shall be able to save frequently ordered items as favorites for quick reordering.​

FR-45: The system shall provide personalized menu recommendations based on order history and preferences.​

Non-Functional Requirements​
Non-functional requirements define system properties and constraints such as reliability, response time, and storage requirements. They apply to the system as a whole and are often more critical than functional requirements, as if they are not met, the system may be useless.​​

Product Requirements - Performance​
NFR-01: The system shall respond to user actions and display menu information within 2 seconds under normal load conditions.​

NFR-02: The system shall load the restaurant menu and complete page rendering in under 1 second.​

NFR-03: The system shall process and confirm order placement within 3 seconds.​

NFR-04: Real-time order status updates shall be reflected in the system within 5 seconds of staff updating the status.​

NFR-05: The search functionality shall return results within 1 second for any query.​

NFR-06: The system shall support at least 500 concurrent users during peak lunch hours without performance degradation.​

NFR-07: The system shall be capable of handling up to 50,000 concurrent orders during peak hours.​

NFR-08: Database queries shall be optimized to return results in less than 500 milliseconds.​

Product Requirements - Reliability​
NFR-09: The system shall have an uptime of at least 99.5% (no more than 3.65 hours downtime per month).​

NFR-10: The system shall maintain high availability with minimal downtime for maintenance (maximum 2 hours per month during off-peak hours).​

NFR-11: The system shall implement automatic backup of all transaction and order data every 24 hours.​

NFR-12: In case of system failure, all pending orders shall be preserved and recovered without data loss.​

NFR-13: The system shall handle errors and exceptions gracefully without crashing, displaying user-friendly error messages.​

NFR-14: The payment processing system shall have 99.9% reliability with transaction rollback capability in case of failures.​

Product Requirements - Usability​
NFR-15: The system shall have an intuitive user interface that requires no training for students to place their first order.​

NFR-16: New users shall be able to complete their first order within 5 minutes of registration.​

NFR-17: The system interface shall be accessible and easy to use for first-time users.​

NFR-18: The system shall follow responsive web design principles, adapting to mobile phones, tablets, and desktop screens.​

NFR-19: The system shall comply with WCAG 2.1 Level AA accessibility standards for users with disabilities.​

NFR-20: All text shall maintain minimum contrast ratios and font sizes for readability.​

NFR-21: The system shall support both English and Arabic language interfaces.​

Product Requirements - Security​​
NFR-22: All user passwords shall be encrypted using industry-standard hashing algorithms (bcrypt or Argon2) before storage.​

NFR-23: All payment information shall be encrypted using SSL/TLS protocols during transmission.​

NFR-24: The system shall comply with PCI-DSS standards for handling credit card information.​

NFR-25: User authentication sessions shall expire after 30 minutes of inactivity.​

NFR-26: The system shall implement role-based access control to restrict administrative functions to authorized staff only.​

NFR-27: All sensitive user data including personal information and order history shall be protected with industry-standard encryption.​

NFR-28: The system shall log all administrative actions for audit purposes.​

NFR-29: The system shall implement protection against common web vulnerabilities (SQL injection, XSS, CSRF).​

NFR-30: Payment processing shall be handled through PCI-compliant third-party payment gateways.​

Product Requirements - Scalability​
NFR-31: The system architecture shall be scalable to accommodate growth in the number of users, menu items, and orders.​

NFR-32: The database design shall support horizontal scaling to handle increased data volume.​

NFR-33: The system shall be able to scale to support multiple cafeteria locations on campus in future releases.​

NFR-34: The infrastructure shall support adding new features without requiring complete system redesign.​

NFR-35: The system shall handle increases in traffic and orders during peak times without compromising performance.​

Organizational Requirements - Standards and Implementation​
NFR-36: The system shall be developed following agile software development methodologies.​​

NFR-37: The system shall be implemented using modern web technologies (HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript frameworks).​

NFR-38: The backend shall be developed using PHP, Node.js, or similar server-side technologies.​

NFR-39: The system shall use MySQL, PostgreSQL, or similar relational database management systems.​

NFR-40: All code shall follow established coding standards and best practices for maintainability.​

NFR-41: The system shall include comprehensive documentation for developers and administrators.​

NFR-42: The system shall undergo thorough testing including unit tests, integration tests, and user acceptance testing.​

Organizational Requirements - Operational​
NFR-43: The system shall be hosted on cloud infrastructure (AWS, Azure, or Google Cloud) for reliability and scalability.​

NFR-44: The system shall provide automated monitoring and alerting for system health and performance issues.​

NFR-45: The system shall maintain detailed logs of all transactions and system events for troubleshooting.​

NFR-46: The system shall support automated deployment and continuous integration/continuous deployment (CI/CD) practices.​

NFR-47: Regular security audits and penetration testing shall be conducted quarterly.​

External Requirements - Compatibility and Integration​
NFR-48: The system shall be compatible with modern web browsers (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge - latest two versions).​

NFR-49: The mobile application shall support iOS 13+ and Android 8.0+ operating systems.​

NFR-50: The system shall integrate with the university's existing student information system for user verification.​

NFR-51: The system shall integrate with third-party payment gateways (Stripe, PayPal, or local payment providers).​

NFR-52: The system shall provide RESTful APIs for potential future integrations with other campus systems.​

NFR-53: The system shall integrate with SMS and email notification services for order updates.​

External Requirements - Data Protection and Privacy​
NFR-54: The system shall comply with relevant data protection regulations (GDPR, local privacy laws).​

NFR-55: User data shall only be used for order processing and system improvement purposes with explicit consent.​

NFR-56: Students shall have the right to view, modify, or delete their personal data in compliance with privacy regulations.​

NFR-57: The system shall retain order history for a maximum of 2 years unless required by regulations.​

NFR-58: The system shall implement data anonymization for analytics and reporting purposes.​

Constraints​
NFR-59: The system development budget shall not exceed the allocated institutional funding.​

NFR-60: The system shall be operational within 6 months from project initiation.​

NFR-61: The system shall operate within AURAK campus network infrastructure constraints.​

NFR-62: Internet connectivity is required for system access - the system assumes users have stable network access.​

NFR-63: The system shall work within the existing cafeteria physical infrastructure and equipment capabilities.
